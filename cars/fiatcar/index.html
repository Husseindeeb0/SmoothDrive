Abarth 500

Abarth is an Italian racing and road car maker and tuner founded by Austrian Carlo Abarth in 1949. Abarth & C. S.p.A. is owned by Stellantis through its Italian subsidiary. Its logo is a shield with a stylized scorpion on a yellow and red background. The Fiat 500 Abarth (Abarth 500) is a more powerful version of the chic Fiat 500 city car, offering a nippy drive in a tiny package, an aggressive bodykit, firmer suspension and plenty of extra horsepower. It rivals the Ford Fiesta ST and MINI Cooper S and features a 1.4-liter engine in various grades of tune. In 2016 the range received a facelift. Abarth models use the 595 name, with 595 Turismo, 595 Competizione, and 695 Biposto models and improved power and acceleration credentials.

Performance Metrics

The Abarth is powered by a 1.4-liter turbocharged engine, but it doesn't feel like it's lacking power—when the turbo kicks in it feels pretty fast on the road, and it's a fun engine to drive with. The Abarth gets a lot of bigger turbos, meaning the exhaust note is hearty and loud, however there's a heap of lag—you need to be on boost before you can really get going, because when you change gear it takes far too long to get into the power band. Try and windy roads are very expensive, but the toned exhaust sounds good.
